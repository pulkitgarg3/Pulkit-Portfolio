Who I Am

With a background spanning electronics engineering, business economics, and analytics, I have worked across research, market analysis, forecasting, and strategy — building a multidisciplinary foundation that I now apply directly to energy systems and transition policy.

My recent work focuses on energy assessment and modelling at TERI, exploring how economies navigate the shift toward cleaner and more resilient energy systems. Before this, I worked with CEEW's power markets team, analysing India's ancillary services market and the economics of re-backdown and energy arbitrage for renewable generators.

"I enjoy turning complex questions into structured insights — using data, economic reasoning, and analytical thinking to support better decision-making across business and public policy."

Outside work, you'll find me on a badminton court, planning my next trip, or following new developments in energy markets, economics, and emerging technologies.
