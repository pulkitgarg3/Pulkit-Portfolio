About

Who I Am

With a background spanning electronics engineering and business economics, I have worked across research, analytics, and strategy. That foundation now sits at the centre of my work in energy modelling and transition policy.

My current work at TERI focuses on decarbonisation and economic modelling, exploring frameworks like CGE modelling and carbon markets to understand how economies shift toward cleaner energy systems. Before this, I worked with CEEW's power markets team on renewable energy integration, curtailment analysis, and climate-economic research.

"I enjoy turning complex questions into structured insights, using data, economic reasoning, and analytical thinking to support better decisions across energy and public policy."

Outside work, you will find me on a badminton court, planning my next trip, or reading about energy markets, climate policy, and economic systems.
